01 · Understanding the Brief

Architecture as argument.

The Hyderabad Ambedkar memorial — opened on 14 April 2023 — places a 125-ft bronze statue atop a three-storey, Parliament-inspired pedestal of roughly 26,258 sq ft on an 11.8-acre campus along Hussain Sagar. The interior houses the galleries addressed by this BOQ. The architecture itself argues a thesis: that Babasaheb's ideas are not memorabilia, they are constitutional infrastructure. Our digital interpretation must respect that thesis.

The Circular Plan

Site drawing sheet B-5-26 (dated 13 April 2026) shows a concentric, ring-shaped plan with 11 numbered zones — display panels, library bookshelves, study set-up, information desks, audio booths, printing-press platform, glass partitions, wooden tables with chairs — radiating around a central core.

This circular geometry should drive the navigation grammar of every kiosk: rings of depth, spokes of theme, a centre that returns the visitor to Babasaheb himself.

Curatorial Principle

Visitors arrive with very different priors — schoolchildren, scholars, activists, international guests, casual tourists, families. The experience must serve all of them without dumbing down or jargonising.

We design for three layers of depth at every touchpoint, allowing each visitor to go as deep as they choose.

Layer 01
Surface
~10 seconds · the hook
  • A vivid image, a quote, a date
  • The visitor decides whether to engage
  • Designed for foot-traffic flow
Layer 02
Story
~90 seconds · the arc
  • Short narrative arc with archival material
  • Motion graphics, audio narration
  • Designed for engaged dwell time
Layer 03
Source
open-ended · the archive
  • Full primary documents
  • Constituent Assembly Debates, BAWS
  • The Constitution itself, multilingual
02 · The Platform

One spine. Many faces.

Every kiosk shares the same problem: navigation, content, attract-mode, accessibility, multilingual switching, analytics, remote update. Building 17 bespoke applications would be slow, fragile, and costly to maintain. Instead we propose a single-spine platform — one codebase, one CMS — with per-kiosk "skins" tuned to each use case.

Kiosk Shell

Electron / Chromium runtime. Auto-restart on crash, watchdog, idle attract-mode, kiosk-lockdown, on-screen-keyboard, accessibility shortcuts. Cross-screen: 27", 32", 55", 65", 85".

ElectronLockdownWatchdog

Experience Layer

React + TypeScript. Per-kiosk modules: timeline, flipbook, multilingual reader, video carousel, photo-booth, testimonial recorder, search, map navigator.

ReactTypeScript

Content CMS

Headless CMS (Strapi / Directus). Authors edit articles, captions, quotes, images, videos in a web admin. One change, instant rollout to all kiosks.

StrapiWeb adminVersion-controlled

Offline-first Delivery

Local cache of all video, audio, image, PDF assets on the mini-PC SSD. CMS pulls deltas only. Works offline if internet drops. Watchdog auto-restarts on power restore.

Local cacheDelta sync

Analytics & Ops

Per-kiosk dashboards: dwell time, taps, language preferences, content popularity. Anonymous and PII-free by default. Remote reboot, update, screenshot, diagnostic logs.

PII-freeRemote ops

Accessibility & Languages

Large-text mode, audio narration, high-contrast theme, captions on every video. Launch: English, Hindi, Telugu. CMS supports RTL (Urdu) and additional Indic scripts via Noto fonts.

A11yEN · हिं · తె+ Urdu, Marathi
Why one platform · 01

Unit cost drops sharply after the first kiosk. BOQ items 8 and 13 explicitly call for replication units; the shared platform makes this trivial.

Why one platform · 02

One brand voice across 26+ screens. Visitors move between galleries; the experience should feel continuous, not disjointed.

Why one platform · 03

Future-proof. Hyderabad will add exhibits, films, anniversaries, language editions. The CMS absorbs this without re-coding.

Why one platform · 04

Lower training load and full audit trail. Museum staff learn one tool. Every content change is version-controlled and reversible — essential for a government cultural institution.

04 · Content Authoring & Creative Media

Software without content is an empty stage.

Our team handles the full content arc — research, scripting, translation, design, animation, sound, narration, photography and film — coordinated with the museum's curatorial committee.

Editorial & Research

We work from primary sources: the published Babasaheb Ambedkar: Writings and Speeches (BAWS) volumes, the Constituent Assembly Debates, gazette archives, the Maharashtra Government's collections, and recognised secondary scholarship — Keer, Omvedt, Jaffrelot, Rodrigues, Anupama Rao.

Every factual claim placed on a kiosk is cited internally and reviewed by the museum's curatorial committee before publication.

Translation & Multilingual

Initial launch in English, Hindi, Telugu. Translation by qualified bilingual writers, then reviewed by a domain expert.

The Constitution kiosks (items 9, 10) use the official Government of India translations to preserve legal-textual fidelity.

Films · Modern India Series (BOQ #2)

Three 3-minute films, 4K, 5.1 audio. Each combines archival material, motion graphics, voice-over, and original score.

Per film: script (3 drafts → curatorial review → lock) · storyboard & animatic · asset gathering or motion-graphic substitution · voice-over in EN / हिं / తె · original music score, sound design, 5.1 mix · DCP / MP4 deliverable + projector calibration on site.

Curved LED Canvases (BOQ #18 & #19)

Item 18 — Ambedkar & the World: a continent-by-continent visual journey of his influence. Item 19 — Universal Rights: a typographic and infographic treatment of the UDHR mapped against Babasaheb's writings on rights and dignity.

Both authored at native curved resolution. Run on scheduled playlists managed by the CMS.

Optional · Photography & Filmmaking

vkstudio.in provides in-house photography and filmmaking. If the museum wishes to commission original portraits of the building, the galleries in use, visitor vignettes, or short documentary pieces for the screens, we deliver these end-to-end without third-party hand-off.

Optional · 360° Wayfinding

A virtual-first wayfinding system: 360° photography of every gallery and lounge, captured at high resolution, stitched into a navigable tour.

Lets visitors — including those with mobility constraints — preview any gallery before walking to it. Lets remote audiences (schools, foreign scholars) visit the museum virtually.

07 · Risk & How We Handle It

The honest risk register.

Curatorial Revisions Late in Production

Lock content at end of Phase 1. Any later revision goes through a change-control note with cost and schedule impact. Avoids the 'death by feedback' pattern common in cultural projects.

Sensitive Interpretation

Babasaheb's legacy is contested terrain. We work only from primary sources or peer-reviewed scholarship. Every interpretive line is reviewed by the museum's curatorial committee before publication.

Hardware-Software Interface Gaps

Joint sign-off matrix with the hardware partner before integration. We do not begin Phase 5 until hardware is on site and powered.

Robotic-arm Safety (BOQ #6)

Interlocks, safe-zone fencing, e-stop, daily warm-up routine. Programmed to refuse operation outside calibrated envelope.

Content Moderation

Testimonial booth recordings publish nothing automatically. Moderation queue in CMS, reviewed by museum staff before any visitor recording appears on other screens.

Privacy

Photo booth & testimonials use explicit on-screen consent. Email delivery only — no print, no internet upload unless visitor opts in. Recordings retained per museum's data-retention policy, deletable on request.

Power-cuts, Network Outages

All content cached locally on the mini-PC. Kiosks operate fully offline if internet drops. Watchdog auto-restarts on hardware power restore.

Schedule Drift

Phases 2 and 3 run in parallel by design. Critical-path items (films) tracked weekly with the curatorial committee. Soft buffers built into Phases 4 and 5.
